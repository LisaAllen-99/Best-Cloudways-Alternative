# Best Cloudways Alternative 2026: Stop Using Cloudways Until You See These 10+ Tested ✅ & Verified Options
![Best Cloudways Alternative](https://blog.cocoslack.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/Best-Cloudways-Alternative-2026.jpeg)
Cloudways is one of the best managed cloud hosting platforms available right now. But it isn't perfect for everyone. If you need built-in email hosting, domain registration, autoscaling without a $100/month budget, or a simpler all-in-one setup — there are better fits out there.

This guide covers 10+ tested alternatives with honest verdicts on who each one is actually for. And if none of them fit your situation better than Cloudways, we'll tell you that too.

## Why People Look for Cloudways Alternatives

Before jumping into the list, it helps to understand the real reasons someone might need a Cloudways alternative:

No built-in email hosting — you need a separate service or the $1/month Rackspace add-on. No domain registration — you manage domains with a separate registrar. Cloudways Autonomous (autoscaling) starts at $100/month — which is too expensive for many smaller sites. No cPanel — users coming from shared hosting may feel disoriented. Not ideal for very low-traffic sites — shared hosting is more cost-effective under 10,000 monthly visitors. And ticket-based support can be inconsistent for complex issues.

If any of those describe your situation, one of the alternatives below may be a better fit. If none of them apply — Cloudways is most likely still your best option.

## 1. Kinsta — Best for Premium Managed WordPress

Starting price: $35/month for 1 site, 10,000 monthly visits

Kinsta runs on Google Cloud's C2 compute-optimized infrastructure and is one of the fastest managed WordPress platforms available. The MyKinsta dashboard is one of the most polished in the industry. Support is 24/7, staffed by WordPress engineers, and responds in under 2 minutes on average.

Where Kinsta genuinely beats Cloudways is in dashboard polish, support consistency, and slightly faster raw server benchmarks. It's a more complete all-in-one package that requires less configuration to get running.

Where it falls short: Kinsta starts at $35/month for just 1 site, with visit-count pricing that pushes you to higher tiers as your traffic grows. Cloudways runs unlimited sites on one flat-cost server. Kinsta also restricts SSH access and maintains a blocked plugin list — limitations Cloudways doesn't impose.

Best for: Users who want the most premium, hands-off managed WordPress experience and have the budget for it. Single high-traffic sites where consistent support matters most.

Stick with Cloudways if: You're running more than one site, your traffic is growing, or you want hosting costs that don't scale with your visitor count.

## 2. WP Engine — Best for Agencies & Enterprise Teams

Starting price: $30/month for 1 site, 25,000 monthly visits

WP Engine is deeply embedded in the agency WordPress ecosystem. It offers dedicated account management on higher tiers, StudioPress and Genesis Framework theme access, and a polished management portal built specifically for teams managing multiple client sites under enterprise contracts.

Where WP Engine stands out: strong agency-specific tooling, very consistent support quality, and a mature ecosystem that agencies have built workflows around for years.

Where it falls short: WP Engine maintains a blocked plugin list — certain caching and security plugins simply aren't allowed. It charges bandwidth overage fees that surprise users on their first invoice. Three sites cost $59/month. And like Kinsta, your bill grows with your traffic whether or not your server is actually under pressure.

Best for: Established agencies with enterprise clients, large WordPress builds where the WP Engine ecosystem has genuine value, and teams where SLA-guaranteed support is a contractual requirement.

Stick with Cloudways if: You're an individual developer or freelancer, you run multiple sites, or you've ever been frustrated by a WP Engine plugin restriction.

## 3. SiteGround — Best for Beginners on a Budget

Starting price: $3.99/month intro (renews at $17.99/month)

SiteGround is one of the most beginner-friendly hosting platforms available. It bundles email hosting, domain management, SSL, and WordPress setup into a single clean interface. For someone launching their very first website with no technical background, SiteGround removes nearly every friction point from the process.

Where SiteGround genuinely wins: everything is in one place — email, domain, hosting, under one login. The onboarding experience is smooth and the support team is patient with non-technical users.

Where it falls short: SiteGround's introductory pricing is a trap. The $3.99/month rate jumps to $17.99/month at renewal — a 4x increase just for staying. Shared hosting also means your resources are shared with other websites, creating a performance ceiling that dedicated cloud hosting doesn't have.

Best for: Absolute beginners launching a personal blog or small business site for the first time who want everything in one place and don't need high performance yet.

Stick with Cloudways if: Your site earns revenue, has real traffic, or is expected to grow. The shared hosting ceiling and renewal pricing make SiteGround expensive long-term for serious sites.

## 4. Flywheel — Best for Designers & Freelancers

Starting price: $30/month for 1 site, 5,000 monthly visits

Flywheel — now part of the WP Engine family — is designed specifically for web designers and creative freelancers who build WordPress sites for clients. It offers a clean client management workflow, the ability to transfer sites to client billing directly, and Local by Flywheel for local WordPress development. The interface is visually polished in a way that design-oriented users tend to prefer.

Where Flywheel stands out: the client handoff workflow is genuinely excellent. Building a site, then transferring billing directly to a client with a few clicks, is something no other platform handles quite as smoothly.

Where it falls short: 5,000 monthly visits on the $30/month entry plan is very low. Flywheel shares some of WP Engine's limitations around flexibility and plugin restrictions.

Best for: Freelance web designers and small agencies who primarily build WordPress sites for clients and value the client handoff workflow over raw performance or cost efficiency.

Stick with Cloudways if: You're running your own sites rather than managing client work, or you need more than 5,000 monthly visitors on an entry plan without paying significantly more.

## 5. Nexcess — Best for WooCommerce Stores

Starting price: $19/month for 1 site

Nexcess (part of Liquid Web) specializes in managed WooCommerce and Magento hosting. Their infrastructure is specifically tuned for ecommerce workloads — high database activity, large product catalogs, complex checkout flows, and order processing at volume. They include WooCommerce-specific performance tools and automatic plugin testing that's particularly valuable for stores running many extensions simultaneously.

Where Nexcess stands out: deep WooCommerce optimization and tooling that goes beyond what a general-purpose managed host provides. Automatic plugin conflict testing before updates is a feature that high-volume store owners genuinely value.

Where it falls short: pricier than Cloudways for comparable resources, less flexibility for non-WooCommerce use cases, and plugin restrictions apply on certain configurations.

Best for: WooCommerce store owners with large product catalogs, high order volumes, or complex plugin stacks where ecommerce-specific optimization justifies the premium price.

Stick with Cloudways if: You're running a standard WooCommerce store under 500,000 monthly visitors. Cloudways' Nginx, Redis, and Object Cache Pro stack handles most WooCommerce workloads excellently at a lower price.

## 6. Pressable — Best for WordPress.com Ecosystem Users

Starting price: $25/month for 1 site, 30,000 monthly visits

Pressable is a managed WordPress host owned by Automattic — the company behind WordPress.com and WooCommerce. It integrates tightly with the WordPress.com ecosystem, Jetpack, and WooCommerce tooling. If your workflow is deeply tied to Automattic's products, Pressable offers more native integration than most other managed hosts.

Where Pressable stands out: the Automattic ownership means tight integration with WordPress.com, Jetpack Security (included on all plans), and WooCommerce tooling. For users already in that ecosystem, it's a natural fit.

Where it falls short: visit-count pricing, less performance flexibility than Cloudways, no cloud provider choice, and a smaller market presence than the major alternatives.

Best for: Users who work heavily within the WordPress.com or Jetpack ecosystem and want hosting that integrates natively with those tools.

Stick with Cloudways if: You don't have a specific dependency on Automattic's ecosystem. Most WordPress users get better performance and more flexibility from Cloudways.

## 7. Vultr — Best for Developers Who Want Raw Infrastructure

Starting price: $6/month for a 1GB instance

Vultr is a cloud infrastructure provider that Cloudways actually uses as one of its backends. If you're a developer who wants the same Vultr NVMe hardware without paying the Cloudways management premium, you can access it directly. Vultr offers High Frequency NVMe servers, bare metal options, and a broader range of compute types than Cloudways exposes through its dashboard.

Where Vultr wins: lower raw server cost, full root access to every layer of the server, and more compute type options than Cloudways exposes.

Where it falls short: everything is manual. WordPress installation, Nginx configuration, caching setup, SSL management, backups, security hardening — all your responsibility. There's no WordPress-specific support, no management dashboard, and no automated anything.

Best for: Developers and sysadmins who want raw Vultr infrastructure with complete control and are comfortable managing everything themselves.

Stick with Cloudways if: You want the same Vultr NVMe hardware with a pre-configured WordPress stack, automated backups, staging, and 24/7 support — without the server management overhead.

## 8. DigitalOcean — Best for Sysadmins and DevOps Teams

Starting price: $6/month for a 1GB Droplet

DigitalOcean is the infrastructure provider that Cloudways is built on. Like Vultr, it offers raw Linux server access at low cost with excellent documentation and a strong developer community. DigitalOcean's App Platform and Managed Databases add some higher-level abstractions, but none provide a managed WordPress experience comparable to Cloudways.

Where DigitalOcean wins: the lowest raw cost, complete root access, excellent documentation, and full infrastructure control for DevOps workflows.

Where it falls short: no WordPress management whatsoever. Support doesn't cover application-level issues. Setup requires real Linux and server administration knowledge. No automated backups, staging, or caching out of the box.

Best for: DevOps engineers and sysadmins building custom server environments, running non-WordPress applications, or managing infrastructure at scale with their own tooling.

Stick with Cloudways if: You want DigitalOcean's infrastructure without managing the server yourself. Cloudways gives you exactly that — the same Droplet hardware with a fully configured WordPress stack on top.

## 9. Linode (Akamai) — Best for Flexible Cloud at Low Cost

Starting price: $5/month for a 1GB instance

Linode — now rebranded as Akamai Cloud — is another cloud infrastructure provider that Cloudways supports as a backend option. It offers competitive pricing, solid performance, and a range of compute options including GPU and high-memory instances. Like Vultr and DigitalOcean, it's raw infrastructure that requires manual configuration for WordPress.

Where Linode wins: lowest raw infrastructure cost in the segment, strong global data center coverage through Akamai's network, and competitive bandwidth pricing.

Where it falls short: same manual management limitations as other raw infrastructure providers. No WordPress-specific tooling or support. Requires server administration expertise.

Best for: Cost-focused developers who want the lowest possible infrastructure cost and have the expertise to manage their own WordPress environment.

Stick with Cloudways if: You want Linode's infrastructure through Cloudways — available as a backend choice — with full managed support and the Cloudways performance stack on top.

## 10. Hostinger Cloud — Best Budget Managed Option

Starting price: $9.99/month

Hostinger offers a managed cloud hosting product that sits between traditional shared hosting and full cloud VPS pricing. It's one of the more affordable managed options available, with a user-friendly dashboard, 24/7 support, and reasonable WordPress performance for small-to-medium sites. Hostinger has invested significantly in performance infrastructure in recent years and now offers a credible budget alternative.

Where Hostinger wins: cheaper entry price than Cloudways, email hosting included, domain registration available, and a very beginner-friendly onboarding experience.

Where it falls short: less flexibility and server-level control than Cloudways, no choice of cloud provider backend, performance lags behind Cloudways on comparable server tiers, and support quality varies. Not recommended for high-traffic or revenue-critical sites.

Best for: Budget-conscious site owners who want a managed environment below the Cloudways entry price and have modest performance requirements.

Stick with Cloudways if: Your site has real traffic, earns revenue, or is growing. The performance and flexibility gap becomes very apparent at 20,000+ monthly visitors.

## 11. RunCloud — Best for Developers Who Want Cloudways-Style Control

Starting price: $12/month panel fee + your own server cost

RunCloud is the closest alternative to Cloudways' actual model. It's a server management panel — not a hosting provider. You bring your own cloud server from any provider, and RunCloud gives you a dashboard to configure Nginx, PHP, MySQL, SSL, deployments, and site management. It's similar to what Cloudways provides but with more granular control and lower total cost for technically capable users.

Where RunCloud wins: lower total cost for developers who can provision their own servers, more granular server configuration access, and compatibility with any cloud provider — not limited to Cloudways' five.

Where it falls short: requires you to provision and manage your own server, no 24/7 WordPress application support, steeper learning curve, and not suitable for non-technical users.

Best for: Developers who want a Cloudways-like management experience with lower cost and more technical control, and are comfortable with their own server provisioning.

Stick with Cloudways if: You want a fully managed experience where the hosting provider handles server provisioning, security patching, and WordPress-level support without you touching the underlying infrastructure.
